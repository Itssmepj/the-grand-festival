Chapter VII — A Letter

Written on your birthday, with more love than these words can hold

My love,

I've been trying to find the right words for weeks. I keep writing sentences and crossing them out because none of them feel big enough. So I'll just say it plainly: you have made my life better in every way that matters.

288 kilometres apart, and you still feel like the closest thing to home. Before you, I thought love was something you waited for. I didn't know it was something that simply arrived and rearranged everything — quietly, completely, permanently.

You are the person I want to call when something wonderful happens, and the person I want to sit in silence with when nothing happens at all. You are the reason ordinary days feel like they deserve to be remembered.

I love how you make space for me, even on the days I take up more room than I should. I love that you are both my comfort and my home.

Today is your day. I hope it feels as beautiful as you make mine feel — every single one of them.

Happy birthday, my love. Here's to every year that comes after this one, and every version of us we haven't met yet.

With all of me,
Always.
